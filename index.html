Easyreadwrite Positive Ageing

Easyreadwrite Workshops are designed to improve the quality of life for people over fifty five by adding another dimension to the range of activities and services on offer for people as they age. Language skills empower and enable us to communicate, to forge links, make connections and express our view of the world we live in.

With Easyreadwrite Workshops Theresa McKenna designs and presents a series of themed workshops which incorporate oral, written and reading activities in an enjoyable and entertaining format to stimulate and engage people as they age. Theresa is an English Teacher. She has been teaching for over thirty years, and is passionate about positive ageing, lifelong learning and literature.

Engaging with language can help prevent and delay the onset of conditions such as Alzheimer’s and Dementia. The social interaction involved in a Workshop helps promote better mental health and wellbeing. Workshops will enrich the lives of the participants through enjoyable language activities, designed around themes. Methodologies and content of the workshops is tailored to suit the individual needs of people in Retirement Villages, Nursing Homes, Day Care Centres, Resource Centres and Community Halls. Memoir, autobiographical writing, biography, short story writing, novel, diary, research, magazine/newspaper article, letter, poetry and drama or other genre can be explored in the workshops.

It is most important that the individual needs of the group are met and they engage in the activities in a way that is comfortable and enjoyable for them. Speaking about our growing older population in Ireland Dr. Stephanie O’Keefe, HSE National Director, Health and Wellbeing referred to tapping into the wealth of knowledge and experience, engaging and capturing the views and experience of our older citizens.

“Tapping into the wealth of knowledge and experience of this key demographic is vital as we aim to put in place services that will efficiently meet the needs of our changing population in the future. With the number of those aged 65 and over projected to increase by 167% to 1.42 million people by 2046, this demographic is growing approximately 20,000 a year and will have increased by 111,200 people in 2022. It is vital we engage and capture their views and experiences.

Theresa McKenna

BA, H Dip, CTG

An English and History teacher for over thirty years, Theresa is passionate about both literacy and literature. During her dynamic career, Theresa has enjoyed working with a variety of mature students on Creative Writing and Reading Workshops. Easy ReadWrite provides Theresa with the opportunity to combine her love of language and desire to help people retain and develop the interest and passion they have in reading and writing as they age.

Theresa has been branching out her work to show her love of literature in many different forms. Some of her work includes numerous short story readings on RTE Radio, participation in features on television, being featured in RTE's September Sundays, a book filled with stories exploring the passions of those who hold Ireland dear, and recently by being the chief editor of two published books:

Where my Memory Takes Me

The fascinating and often touching memories of the late Annette O'Brien, a beloved community figure in Kells, Meath and a dear friend to Theresa

Celebrating Forty Years of Steam Threshing in Moynalty

A book which is dear to Theresa's heart as someone who truly loves the village of Moynalty and it's storied history
